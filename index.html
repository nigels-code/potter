The Chatty Potter

Julie, a Ceramic Artist works from her home studio in Crookes, Sheffield. Mainly using stoneware clay with its durability and usability in household wares. She Creates plant pots, vases, plates, pet bowls as well as other unique commissions. She also teaches workshops and these centre around well-being and mindfulness.
Handbuilding is an ancient pottery-making technique that mainly focuses around pinching, coil building, and slab building. I use all three methods in my ceramic work but in my workshops I tend to use coil building the most. I find it very therapeutic as it's using hands, fingers and simple tools. To make a pinch pot you would use your thumb and put it into a ball of clay and pinch the clay between your thumb and fingers while rotating the ball of clay. This thins out the sides and raises the height of the pot. Pinching can be very rustic and organic, but can also be so unique and personal. Coil building is a forming method that uses sausage/ropelike coils of clay, assembled in rings to build up the wall of a vessel or pot.
